Lampshades
I decided to create these lampshades using data from the E-Redes energy consumption consultation platform. Each lampshade represents the daily energy consumption of a household over the course of a month. The openings in the pieces correspond to the days of the month and reflect the geometry associated with that day’s energy consumption range. The design principle behind this choice is that lower energy consumption results in larger openings, thereby “rewarding” households with reduced consumption by allowing more light to pass through the lampshade.

To ensure more balanced results, the household’s total energy consumption was divided by the number of household members. This project also combines generative design with ceramic 3D printing.
